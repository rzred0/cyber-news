# Cybersecurity News - March 10, 2026

## Major Breaches
* **Ericsson US Discloses Data Breach**: Ericsson Inc. confirmed that attackers stole data belonging to employees and customers following a hack on one of its service providers.
* **ShinyHunters Claims Salesforce Aura Data Theft**: The ShinyHunters extortion gang claims to be actively exploiting a new bug in misconfigured Salesforce Experience Cloud platforms to steal data from instances.
* **Microsoft Teams Phishing**: Hackers are contacting financial and healthcare employees over Teams to deploy the new A0Backdoor malware via Quick Assist remote access.

## AI in Security
* **Malicious npm Package Posing as OpenClaw Installer**: A fake OpenClaw installer ("@openclaw-ai/openclawai") was uploaded to npm, deploying a persistent RAT to steal macOS credentials, browser data, and crypto wallets.
* **OpenClaw Vulnerabilities**: Reports indicate RCE risks, leaked tokens, and over 21,000 exposed OpenClaw AI agent instances connected to Slack, Gmail, and Drive.

## APTs
* **UNC4899 (North Korea)**: This state-sponsored actor breached a crypto firm after a developer AirDropped a trojanized file to a work device. The attackers pivoted to the cloud using living-off-the-cloud (LOTC) techniques to steal millions.
* **CL-UNK-1068 (China)**: A Chinese threat actor has targeted high-value critical infrastructure in Asia (aviation, energy, government) using custom malware, web server exploits, and Mimikatz for cyber espionage.
* **Russian State-Sponsored Phishing**: The Dutch government warned of ongoing Signal and WhatsApp account hijacking attacks targeting government officials, military personnel, and journalists.

## Policy & Legal
* **EU Court Adviser on Phishing Refunds**: An adviser to the Court of Justice of the EU (CJEU) issued a formal opinion suggesting that banks must immediately refund account holders affected by unauthorized transactions, even when the victim is at fault.
* **FBI Warning on Official Impersonation**: The FBI issued a warning about criminals impersonating U.S. city and county officials in phishing attacks targeting entities requesting planning and zoning permits.
