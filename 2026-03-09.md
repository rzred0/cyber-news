# Cybersecurity News - 2026-03-09

### 🚨 Major Breaches & Vulnerabilities
* **Cognizant TriZetto Breach:** A breach at TriZetto Provider Solutions has exposed the health data of 3.4 million patients.
* **CISA Warning on iOS Flaws:** CISA has ordered U.S. federal agencies to patch three iOS security flaws that are actively being targeted in cyberespionage and crypto-theft attacks.
* **FBI Systems Breach:** The FBI confirmed it's investigating a breach affecting systems used to manage surveillance and wiretap warrants.
* **Bing AI Promoting Fake OpenClaw Malware:** Microsoft's Bing AI-enhanced search promoted fake OpenClaw GitHub repositories that instruct users to run commands deploying info-stealing malware.
* **Wikipedia Vandalism Worm:** The Wikimedia Foundation suffered a security incident involving a self-propagating JavaScript worm that vandalized pages across multiple wikis.

### 🤖 AI in Security & Threat Tactics
* **OpenAI Codex Security Agent:** OpenAI started rolling out a new AI-powered security agent that scanned 1.2 million commits and found over 10,500 high-severity vulnerabilities during its beta.
* **Hackers Abusing AI at Every Stage:** Microsoft released a report noting that threat actors are increasingly using AI to accelerate attacks, scale malicious activity, and lower technical barriers across all stages of cyberattacks.
* **Transparent Tribe's "Vibeware":** A Pakistan-aligned threat actor is using AI coding tools to mass-produce disposable, multi-language malware implants targeting India.
* **Fake Claude Code Install Guides:** Attackers are using the "ClickFix" social engineering technique to push info-stealers by tricking users with fake installation guides for legitimate CLI tools.

### 🕵️ Advanced Persistent Threats (APTs)
* **MuddyWater Targets U.S. Networks:** An Iran-linked hacking group (MuddyWater) has been found embedding itself in U.S. networks—including banks, airports, and defense suppliers—deploying a new "Dindoor" backdoor.
* **China-Linked Hackers Targeting Telecoms:** A group tracked as UAT-9244 is targeting critical telecommunications infrastructure in South America with three different implants (TernDoor, PeerTime, and BruteEntry).

### 🏛️ Policy & Legal
* **EU Court on Phishing Refunds:** An Advocate General of the Court of Justice of the EU suggested that banks must immediately refund account holders affected by unauthorized transactions (like phishing), even if the account holder was at fault.
* **$46M Crypto Theft Arrest:** A U.S. government contractor's son was arrested in Saint Martin for allegedly stealing over $46 million in cryptocurrency from the U.S. Marshals Service.
